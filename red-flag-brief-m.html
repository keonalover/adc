The five patterns
$4,800/mo

Vendor pricing gap Red flag Stores B, C, D

Store A's invoices are the benchmark. B, C, and D pay above it on the same Sysco items, Store C the most. Same SKUs, higher prices, never reconciled across the group.

Where to start Take Store A's pricing to the Sysco rep and consolidate B, C, and D to that benchmark, starting with your three biggest leakage items: 80/20 ground beef, chicken thighs, and roma tomatoes.

$2,125/mo

Discount drift Red flag Store C

Store C's comp rate sits at 5.8% against a 4.1% target, the highest in the group, with 47 manager-override comps last month and no approval step in the POS.

Where to start Lock comps behind manager approval at Store C, then review the override checks.

$3,100/mo

Weekday labor overage Watch Stores A, B

On soft weekdays, labor runs about 35% of sales against a 28% target, roughly $775 of overage a week, almost every week.

Where to start On Monday through Wednesday at A and B, schedule one closer for a 5-hour shift instead of 8, starting at 6pm rather than 3pm, so the full crew isn't on the clock through a slow close.

$1,735/mo

Inventory shrinkage Watch Store D

The gap between what the recipes say should be on hand and the physical count is widening reconciliation to reconciliation, concentrated in two proteins: chicken thighs and 80/20 ground beef both come up short every count, with the latest the worst yet.

Where to start Count chicken thighs and 80/20 ground beef at Store D daily for two weeks against the receiving logs and waste sheets to find where they're walking.

$690/mo

After-hours voids Control risk Store B

Store B logged 35 voids after close, concentrated in two employee IDs with no reason codes. The flag here is control, not just the dollars.

Where to start Pull Store B's after-hours voids by employee, require reason codes, and treat it as a control review first.
